# In-app QR scanning decodes and navigates, reproducing the camera app

A **not-set-up [Participant](../../CONTEXT.md)** (the "Keine Rolle festgelegt" stub) and a team **[Member](../../CONTEXT.md)** can scan a QR from inside the app instead of leaving for the phone's camera app. A "QR scannen" button opens the camera in the existing [Modal](0029-modal-has-an-always-visible-close-button.md); on a successful read the scanner does exactly what the camera app does — **decode the QR and navigate to the decoded URL** — and the existing `/setup-team`, `/setup-agent`, and `/find` pages then do all the role-specific work ([Registration](../../CONTEXT.md), [Confirmation](../../CONTEXT.md), [Find](../../CONTEXT.md)). The feature is purely additive: nothing about backend QR minting changes, and the camera-app path stays as the fallback when in-app scanning fails (permission denied, no camera, no read).

A reader might expect the scanner to *handle the result inline* — e.g. record a [Find](../../CONTEXT.md) in place without a page change. We deliberately don't: decode-and-navigate keeps the scanner a thin component, reuses every existing flow and its validation, and means the in-app path and the camera-app fallback land on the identical pages. Agents are out of scope — once set up an [Agent](../../CONTEXT.md) only ever *presents* a [Find QR](../../CONTEXT.md), never scans one, which is why the user story names "team or not-setup participant," not the agent.

Consequences:
- **The scanner does not validate the decoded URL before navigating** (no same-origin or path allowlist). The accepted risk: a QR encoding an arbitrary URL — including an off-domain phishing page or another [Tenant](../../CONTEXT.md)'s domain — will be followed from inside the app, an open redirect the native camera app also has but that we are choosing not to guard against. This was weighed explicitly against adding a same-origin + `/setup-team|/setup-agent|/find` allowlist and decided in favour of reproducing the camera app's behaviour verbatim. Recorded so a reviewer sees the missing guard is a conscious decision, not an oversight; revisit here if the risk posture changes.
- Because the decoded value is an arbitrary absolute URL (no same-origin guard), navigation is a full page load via `window.location.href = decoded`, **not** a client-side `router.push` — even for a legitimate same-origin `/find` or `/setup-*` link. A clean in-app `router.push` would have required the same-origin check we declined above.
- `getUserMedia` requires a secure context — fine in prod (HTTPS behind traefik) and on `localhost`.
